<p>Mental health podcasts have gone from niche to essential. Whether you want research-backed strategies from psychologists, raw conversations about what it actually means to be human, or practical tools you can use today, these ten shows deliver. All are actively releasing new episodes in 2026.</p>

{% include callToActionRow.html %}

<br>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Hidden+Brain">1. Hidden Brain</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/hidden-brain.jpg" alt="Hidden Brain podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Shankar Vedantam explores the unconscious patterns that drive human behaviour, drawing on psychology, neuroscience and economics. Each episode takes a single idea and unpacks it through storytelling and research in a way that genuinely changes how you think. It's the rare podcast that makes academic science feel like a page-turner.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Happiness+Lab">2. The Happiness Lab</a></h3>
<p>Yale professor Dr. Laurie Santos takes the science from her wildly popular course on happiness and turns it into something you can actually use. She challenges common assumptions about what makes people happy and backs everything with peer-reviewed research. If you've ever wondered why getting what you want doesn't feel as good as you expected, start here.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Unlocking+Us">3. Unlocking Us</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/unlocking-us.jpg" alt="Unlocking Us podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Brene Brown is back after a hiatus, releasing quarterly deep-dive series on vulnerability, courage and human connection. Her research on shame and empathy has shaped how millions of people think about emotional health. The conversations are honest, grounded in data and never preachy.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Therapy+for+Black+Girls">4. Therapy for Black Girls</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/therapy-for-black-girls.jpg" alt="Therapy for Black Girls podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Dr. Joy Harden Bradford, a licensed psychologist in Atlanta, hosts weekly conversations about mental health, personal development and becoming your best self. The show covers everything from setting boundaries to navigating relationships, with a warm and accessible approach. It's built a genuine community, not just an audience.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=On+Purpose+with+Jay+Shetty">5. On Purpose with Jay Shetty</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/on-purpose-jay-shetty.jpg" alt="On Purpose with Jay Shetty podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Jay Shetty blends insights from his time as a monk with interviews featuring psychologists, authors and thought leaders. The focus is on purpose, emotional intelligence and building habits that support your mental wellbeing. Episodes are concise enough to fit into a morning routine, which is part of the appeal.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=We+Can+Do+Hard+Things">6. We Can Do Hard Things</a></h3>
<p>Glennon Doyle, her sister Amanda and wife Abby Wambach have honest, unscripted conversations about the things most people avoid talking about. They cover parenting, identity, relationships and mental health with a mix of humour and real vulnerability. It feels like sitting in on a conversation between people who've stopped pretending everything is fine.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Where+Should+We+Begin%3F">7. Where Should We Begin?</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/where-should-we-begin.jpg" alt="Where Should We Begin? podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Therapist Esther Perel brings listeners into real (anonymised) therapy sessions with couples working through conflict, intimacy and communication. You hear the actual dynamics play out in real time, which is far more illuminating than any advice column. Even if you're not in a relationship, you'll learn something about yourself.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Mel+Robbins+Podcast">8. The Mel Robbins Podcast</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/the-mel-robbins-podcast.jpg" alt="The Mel Robbins Podcast podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Mel Robbins delivers science-backed strategies for breaking unhelpful thought patterns and taking action. Her style is direct and no-nonsense, covering confidence, motivation and emotional resilience with practical tools you can apply immediately. It's one of the most shared podcasts in the world for a reason.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Feel+Better%2C+Live+More">9. Feel Better, Live More</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/feel-better-live-more.jpg" alt="Feel Better, Live More podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Dr. Rangan Chatterjee, one of the UK's most recognised GPs, takes a whole-person approach to mental and physical wellbeing. He interviews leading experts on sleep, nutrition, movement and emotional health, always bringing it back to what you can actually change in your daily life. Over 600 episodes deep and still consistently excellent.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Mental+Illness+Happy+Hour">10. Mental Illness Happy Hour</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-mental-health-podcasts/mental-illness-happy-hour.jpg" alt="Mental Illness Happy Hour podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Paul Gilmartin creates a space where people talk openly about depression, trauma, addiction and the full spectrum of mental illness. Guests range from comedians to therapists to everyday people sharing experiences they rarely discuss out loud. It's raw, sometimes heavy, and one of the most genuinely helpful mental health resources in podcasting.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">
