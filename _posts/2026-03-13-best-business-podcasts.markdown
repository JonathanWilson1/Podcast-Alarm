<p>Good business podcasts do something rare: they make you think differently about work without wasting your time. These 10 shows are the ones actually worth subscribing to in 2026 — each one run by people who know what they're talking about.</p>

{% include callToActionRow.html %}

<br>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Acquired">1. Acquired</a></h3>
<p>Ben Gilbert and David Rosenthal break down the stories behind the world's greatest companies — Nike, LVMH, Costco, Formula 1. Episodes run 3-4 hours and feel like 30 minutes. The research is absurdly thorough, and they release just one or two episodes a month, so every one counts.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=How+I+Built+This">2. How I Built This</a></h3>
<p>Guy Raz interviews founders about how they actually built their companies — the failures, the lucky breaks, the moments they nearly quit. With over 800 episodes covering everyone from the founders of Airbnb to Raising Cane's, it's one of the most consistently excellent business shows running.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=My+First+Million">3. My First Million</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/my-first-million.jpg" alt="My First Million podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Sam Parr and Shaan Puri riff on business ideas, trends, and opportunities with infectious energy. It's fast, funny, and genuinely useful — they spot trends early and break down why they work. Over 800 episodes in and the ideas keep coming.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Diary+of+a+CEO">4. The Diary of a CEO</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/diary-of-a-ceo.jpg" alt="The Diary of a CEO podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Steven Bartlett gets guests to go deeper than they do on any other show. The long-form interviews cover business, psychology, health, and leadership with a directness that cuts through the usual PR answers. It's the number one podcast in the UK for a reason.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=All-In">5. All-In</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/all-in-podcast.jpg" alt="All-In podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Chamath Palihapitiya, Jason Calacanis, David Sacks, and David Friedberg — four Silicon Valley investors arguing about tech, markets, and politics every week. You won't agree with everything, but you'll think harder about all of it. The chemistry between the hosts is what keeps people coming back.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Masters+of+Scale">6. Masters of Scale</a></h3>
<p>LinkedIn co-founder Reid Hoffman talks to founders and CEOs about how they scaled their companies. Each episode is built around a counterintuitive insight — like why doing things that don't scale is exactly how you scale. Well-produced and genuinely strategic.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=WorkLife+with+Adam+Grant">7. WorkLife with Adam Grant</a></h3>
<p>Organisational psychologist Adam Grant explores how to make work not suck. He backs up every episode with real research, not just opinions, and covers everything from rethinking meetings to finding meaning in your job. Recent guests include Esther Perel and Matt Damon.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Lenny%27s+Podcast">8. Lenny's Podcast</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/lennys-podcast.jpg" alt="Lenny's Podcast podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Lenny Rachitsky interviews product leaders, growth experts, and startup founders with a depth you won't find elsewhere. If you build products for a living, this is required listening. Over 330 episodes packed with frameworks and tactics you can actually use on Monday morning.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=We+Study+Billionaires">9. We Study Billionaires</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/we-study-billionaires.jpg" alt="We Study Billionaires podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>The Investor's Podcast Network's flagship show breaks down the strategies of the world's most successful investors. Hosts Stig Brodersen, Clay Finck, and others release three episodes a week covering value investing, stock analysis, and financial history. Over 180 million downloads and growing.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Indicator+from+Planet+Money">10. The Indicator from Planet Money</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-business-podcasts/indicator-planet-money.jpg" alt="The Indicator from Planet Money podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>NPR's daily business podcast explains one economic concept in 10 minutes or less. It's the most efficient way to understand what's happening in the economy without a finance degree. Perfect if you want to sound smart in meetings without committing to hour-long episodes.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">
