<p>Sports talk radio used to be your only option between games. Now podcasts do it better — deeper analysis, funnier hosts, and you can listen whenever you want. Here are 10 worth subscribing to, covering everything from the Premier League to the NBA to the F1 grid.</p>

{% include callToActionRow.html %}

<br>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Bill+Simmons+Podcast">1. The Bill Simmons Podcast</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/bill-simmons-podcast.jpg" alt="The Bill Simmons Podcast podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Bill Simmons has been doing this longer than almost anyone, and it shows. He covers the NBA, NFL, and pop culture with a mix of genuine expertise and fan energy that feels like talking sports with your smartest mate. Episodes drop several times a week, so there's always something fresh.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=That+Peter+Crouch+Podcast">2. That Peter Crouch Podcast</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/peter-crouch-podcast.jpg" alt="That Peter Crouch Podcast podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Former England striker Peter Crouch, BBC journalist Tom Fordyce, and radio host Chris Stark talk football with the kind of behind-the-scenes honesty you rarely hear from ex-players. Crouch is genuinely hilarious and surprisingly candid about dressing room culture, transfers, and what life is really like as a professional footballer.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=F1%3A+Beyond+the+Grid">3. F1: Beyond the Grid</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/f1-beyond-the-grid.jpg" alt="F1: Beyond the Grid podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>The official Formula 1 podcast, hosted by Tom Clarkson. Each episode is a long-form interview with a driver, team principal, or key figure from the paddock. You get stories and insights that never make it into the TV coverage — essential listening during race season.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Lowe+Post">4. The Lowe Post</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/the-lowe-post.jpg" alt="The Lowe Post podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Zach Lowe is one of the most respected basketball writers alive, and his podcast matches the quality of his writing. Deep tactical breakdowns, honest trade analysis, and interviews with coaches and front office people who actually know what they're talking about. If you care about the NBA beyond the highlight reels, this is your show.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Tailenders">5. Tailenders</a></h3>
<p>Greg James, Jimmy Anderson, and Felix White somehow turned cricket into one of the funniest podcasts going. They cover Test cricket, village cricket, and everything in between with genuine warmth and running jokes that reward long-time listeners. You don't even need to like cricket to enjoy it — but you will after a few episodes.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Athletic+Football+Podcast">6. The Athletic Football Podcast</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/athletic-football-podcast.jpg" alt="The Athletic Football Podcast podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Daily coverage of the Premier League, Champions League, and European football from some of the best journalists in the business. The analysis is sharp without being dry, and the reporting breaks actual news. If you follow football seriously, this should be in your feed.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Pardon+My+Take">7. Pardon My Take</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/pardon-my-take.jpg" alt="Pardon My Take podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Big Cat and PFT Commenter have built the biggest sports comedy podcast in the US for a reason. They cover the NFL, NBA, and college sports with a mix of satire, genuine analysis, and athlete interviews that are far more relaxed than the usual media circuit. Three episodes a week and consistently entertaining.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Rugby+Pod">8. The Rugby Pod</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/the-rugby-pod.jpg" alt="The Rugby Pod podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Former England internationals Andy Goode and Jim Hamilton bring the same energy to rugby analysis that you'd get at the pub after a match — loud opinions, proper knowledge, and plenty of laughs. They cover the Six Nations, Premiership, and international rugby with guests from across the sport.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=No+Laying+Up">9. No Laying Up</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-sports-podcasts/no-laying-up.jpg" alt="No Laying Up podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Golf podcasts can be stuffy. No Laying Up is the opposite. Their team covers the PGA Tour, majors, and golf culture with a fan-first perspective that manages to be both knowledgeable and fun. The travel content — where they play famous courses around the world — is brilliant too.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=The+Old+Man+and+the+Three">10. The Old Man and the Three</a></h3>
<p>JJ Redick brings 15 years of NBA experience to every conversation, and it makes a difference. His interviews with current and former players go places that media-trained press conferences never do. The tactical breakdowns are clear enough for casual fans but detailed enough for basketball obsessives.</p>
