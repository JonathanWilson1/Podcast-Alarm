<p>The best walks need the right company. Not something loud or distracting — something that matches your pace, holds your attention without demanding it, and makes you want to keep going a little further. These ten podcasts do exactly that.</p>

{% include callToActionRow.html %}

<br>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Radiolab">1. Radiolab</a></h3>
<p>Hosted by Lulu Miller and Latif Nasser, Radiolab investigates big ideas through science, philosophy and storytelling. Episodes unfold at a pace that rewards attention without rushing you — perfect for a long walk where your mind is already open to wondering about things. The sound design is some of the best in podcasting.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Heavyweight">2. Heavyweight</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-podcasts-for-walking/heavyweight.jpg" alt="Heavyweight podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Jonathan Goldstein helps real people resolve something unfinished from their past. Each episode is funny, surprising and quietly moving. The stories are self-contained and run about 40 minutes, which is just right for a morning walk around the neighbourhood.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Ramblings">3. Ramblings</a></h3>
<p>Clare Balding walks through the British countryside with a different guest each episode, having conversations about life, nature and whatever comes up along the way. It's literally a walking podcast — recorded on footpaths and trails across the UK. Hard to think of a better match for your own walk.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=99%25+Invisible">4. 99% Invisible</a></h3>
<p>Roman Mars explores the design and architecture of the everyday world — the things you walk past without noticing. Episodes cover everything from park benches to city grids to the reason pavements look the way they do. Once you start listening, you'll see your surroundings differently.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Cautionary+Tales">5. Cautionary Tales</a></h3>
<p>Tim Harford, the Financial Times columnist, tells true stories of mistakes, disasters and unintended consequences. Each episode is a self-contained narrative that runs around 30-40 minutes and leaves you with something to think about for the rest of your walk. The storytelling is meticulous without ever feeling heavy.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=You%27re+Wrong+About">6. You're Wrong About</a></h3>
<p>Sarah Marshall re-examines events and people that the public got wrong — moral panics, misunderstood figures, oversimplified scandals. The research is deep but the tone is warm and conversational, like catching up with someone who happens to know a lot about the thing you're discussing.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Stuff+You+Should+Know">7. Stuff You Should Know</a></h3>
<p>Josh Clark and Chuck Bryant have been explaining how things work since 2008, and somehow it never gets old. Episodes cover everything from how landfills work to the history of postcards. The chemistry between the two hosts is genuinely easy to listen to — it's the podcast equivalent of an unhurried conversation.</p>

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Philosophize+This%21">8. Philosophize This!</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-podcasts-for-walking/philosophize-this.jpg" alt="Philosophize This! podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Stephen West makes philosophy accessible without dumbing it down. Episodes run 20-30 minutes and cover thinkers from Aristotle to the present day in a way that feels like a friendly lecture from someone who actually cares about the subject. Ideal walking length, and you'll arrive home feeling like you've properly used your brain.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Everything+Everywhere+Daily">9. Everything Everywhere Daily</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-podcasts-for-walking/everything-everywhere-daily.jpg" alt="Everything Everywhere Daily podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Gary Arndt delivers a single fascinating topic in about 10-15 minutes. History, science, geography, culture — all researched properly and presented cleanly. If you only have time for a short walk, this is the one. Stack two or three episodes and you'll have learned something genuinely interesting before you're back at your door.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">

<h3><a class="text-info" href="https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/search?term=Ten+Percent+Happier">10. Ten Percent Happier</a></h3>
<img src="/images/blog/charts/best-podcasts-for-walking/ten-percent-happier.jpg" alt="Ten Percent Happier podcast artwork" width="100" style="border-radius:12px;float:left;margin:0 15px 15px 0;">
<p>Dan Harris talks to meditation teachers, neuroscientists and psychologists about practical ways to be less stressed and more present. The conversations are grounded and sceptical — Harris is a journalist, not a guru. Walking is already good for your head; pairing it with this podcast makes it even better.</p>
<br style="clear:both;">
